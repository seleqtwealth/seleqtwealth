Welcome. This is a practical assessment of how you handle the operational side of the job, onboarding, documentation, accuracy and getting things done, not a theory exam. We want to see how you work in real situations on the desk.

The last section, On the Job, puts you in real operational situations and asks how you would handle them. There are no trick questions and no negative marking. If you are unsure, give your best practical reasoning rather than leaving it blank.

Take your time. Aim for roughly 30 minutes. When you are done, press Finish & Lock at the bottom.
Section A

Onboarding & KYC

5 questions
A1KYC documentsMultiple choice

You're opening a folio for a new resident individual. Which is the correct, complete set to collect for KYC?

Correct answer
A. Identity (PAN), photo, address proof and bank proof are all needed up front. B under-collects (bank proof is needed for payouts); C is a misconception (Aadhaar helps with ID/address but you still need PAN, photo and bank proof); D gathers irrelevant documents.
A2KYC processMultiple choice

A new client's KYC is processed. Where do the records get registered so the KYC is recognised industry-wide?

Correct answer
A. KYC is registered with a KRA / CKYC, which makes it portable and recognised across SEBI-registered intermediaries. The others sound plausible but aren't where industry-wide KYC sits.
A3KYC portabilityTrue / False · why

Once a client is KYC-compliant through a KRA, they generally don't need to repeat full KYC to invest through a new intermediary.

Answer
True. KYC done with a KRA / CKYC is portable across SEBI-registered intermediaries, so you don't redo full KYC, you only collect any firm-specific or incremental forms (FATCA, additional details, fresh nomination). Look for: True + the KRA / portability reasoning.
A4Special casesMultiple choice

You're setting up an investment for a minor. Which is correct?

Correct answer
A. A minor's folio is in the minor's sole name, operated by the natural/legal guardian, until the minor turns 18 (then it's converted to major status). No joint holder is allowed (B), it isn't held in the guardian's name (C), and the minor can't transact directly (D).
A5Family onboardingWritten

A new HNI client wants to onboard himself, his wife, and the family HUF. From an onboarding standpoint, what would you set up, and what documents would you gather for each?

What to look for
Treat each as a separate holder/entity with its own KYC and folio: individual KYC for him and his wife (PAN, address proof, photo, bank proof, FATCA, nomination). The HUF is a distinct entity, its own PAN, the HUF deed/declaration, and the Karta's details/KYC. Reward recognising three separate entities, each with its own KYC, bank mandate and nomination, not lumping them together.
Section B

Documentation & Accuracy

5 questions
B1Spot the issueMultiple choice

A purchase application carries these four details. Which one would get it rejected?

Correct answer
A. The name must match the PAN exactly, "R. K. Sharma" vs "Rajesh Kumar Sharma" is a mismatch that gets rejected. B, C and D are all correct and expected. Tests whether they actually read the details.
B2Cheque accuracyMultiple choice

You're banking a client's cheque for a lump-sum investment. Which of these would get the cheque returned?

Correct answer
A. The figures (1,00,000) and the words (one lakh ten thousand) don't match, so the cheque is returned. B, C and D are all fine. Tests careful reading.
B3CheckingTrue / False · why

Once you've carefully filled in a client's form yourself, having a second person check it before submission is an unnecessary delay.

Answer
False. A second review (maker-checker) catches errors before they cause rejections, rework and delays for the client, accuracy is worth the few minutes, especially on anything financial or compliance-related. Look for: False + the value of a double-check / maker-checker.
B4Records · why it mattersWritten

A client phones and asks you to update their registered mobile number and email on their folios. How should this be handled, and why does it matter to do it carefully?

What to look for
Put it through the proper change-of-details process with verification of the client's identity and the right authorisation, and keep a record. Why it matters: registered contact details are a fraud vector, whoever controls the mobile/email gets the OTPs, statements and transaction alerts, so an unverified change can let a fraudster intercept communications or route a redemption. Reward the verified process and the fraud-risk awareness, not just "update it."
B5Spot the mistakesWritten

You're checking a new client's onboarding form before submission. It shows: applicant name "Sunil R Patel"; PAN card reads "Sunil Rameshbhai Patel"; the bank IFSC has only 9 characters; the nominee field is blank; and the FATCA declaration is unsigned. What would you flag before submitting?

What to look for · attention to detail
Catch all of these: (1) the name doesn't match the PAN, it must read exactly as on the PAN, or it'll be rejected; (2) the IFSC is the wrong length (a valid IFSC is 11 characters), so the bank details are invalid; (3) the nominee is blank, it should be filled or nomination explicitly opted out; (4) the FATCA declaration is unsigned, a mandatory signature is missing. Reward catching most or all, and knowing each would cause a rejection or compliance gap.
Section C

Operations & Coordination

5 questions
C1Cut-off timingMultiple choice

A client wants today's NAV for a ₹20 lakh equity fund purchase. For today's NAV to apply, what has to happen?

Correct answer
A. For a large purchase, both the application and the realised (cleared) funds must be in and time-stamped before the cut-off. The common mistakes are thinking the form alone (B, D) or the money alone (C) is enough.
C2Who does whatMultiple choice

To check the status of a client's mutual fund transaction or pull a folio statement, you'd typically go to:

Correct answer
A. RTAs (CAMS, KFintech) handle MF transaction processing, folio servicing and statements. The custodian, exchange and the client's bank all exist in the chain but aren't where you service a folio.
C3Follow-through · adviseWritten

You've been chasing the RTA/AMC for three days about a client's pending transaction with no resolution, and the client is getting anxious. How do you handle it, both the operational side and the client?

What to look for
Operationally: keep escalating through the proper channels (RTA escalation desk, the firm's AMC relationship/RM), pin down the exact blocker, and document the follow-ups. Client side: stay proactive, update them with the honest status and a realistic expectation rather than going silent, and loop in your senior/RM if it stays stuck. Reward persistence + escalation + proactive, honest communication, not just "wait and hope."
C4SettlementTrue / False · why

A client redeeming from an equity mutual fund should be told the money will take a few working days to reach their bank account, not the same day.

Answer
True. Equity fund redemptions settle over a few working days (T+ a couple of days); liquid/overnight funds are faster. Set that expectation up front so the client isn't surprised. Look for: True + awareness of the settlement cycle.
C5Prioritise · coordinateWritten

It's a busy morning: a client's SIP mandate has just failed, an RM needs a portfolio statement for an 11 am client meeting, and a redemption request must go in before the afternoon cut-off. How would you prioritise and handle all three?

What to look for · operational judgment
Prioritise by hard deadline and impact: the statement for the 11 am meeting and the redemption before the cut-off are time-boxed, do those first (and confirm the cut-off time). The failed SIP needs fixing and the client informed, but it isn't a hard deadline and can be actioned right after. Reward reasoning about deadlines, communicating with the RM and client, and not dropping any of the three.
Section D

On the Job

5 situations
D1SIP failureWritten

A client's monthly SIP has failed because of an insufficient-balance / mandate issue. What do you do?

What to look for
Check the exact reason, inform the client promptly and tactfully, help fix it (ensure funds / re-activate or re-register the mandate so the next cycle goes through), and reassure them that a single missed SIP is just a missed installment, not a default or penalty. Reward: diagnose → communicate → fix → follow-up.
D2Rejection fixWritten

You submitted a new client's onboarding and the KRA/RTA rejected it citing a "name mismatch." Walk through how you'd resolve it.

What to look for
Identify the mismatch (the form/records vs the name exactly as on the PAN), get the correct details or proof from the client, correct the form/records to match the PAN precisely, resubmit, and confirm clearance, keeping the client informed throughout. Reward a methodical fix and not just blindly resubmitting.
D3Judgment · escalateWritten

A client asks you to process a redemption into a different person's bank account (not their registered one) "just this once, to save time." What do you do?

What to look for · integrity
Don't do it. Redemptions go only to the client's own registered, verified bank account, this is a basic anti-fraud / compliance control. Explain politely, offer the proper route (register and verify the account first if it genuinely belongs to the client), and escalate to a senior/compliance if pressured. Reward refusing the bypass, the compliance reasoning, and still being helpful. A willingness to bend the rule here is a serious red flag.
D4DiscrepancyWritten

While preparing a client's portfolio statement, you notice a transaction in the records that the client insists they never made. What do you do?

What to look for
Take it seriously, don't dismiss or assume. Verify against the source records (RTA/AMC), check for a data-entry error or a folio mix-up, and if it looks like an unauthorised or genuinely unexplained transaction, escalate to the RM/compliance, document everything, and keep the client informed. Reward verifying first + escalating an unauthorised transaction appropriately rather than ignoring it.
D5Own mistake · integrityWritten

Late in the afternoon you realise you submitted a client's purchase for ₹5,00,000 when they had instructed ₹50,000, and it's already gone to the RTA. What do you do?

What to look for · ownership under pressure
Own it immediately, no hiding or delaying. Act fast: check whether it can still be stopped/reversed given the cut-off and the RTA process; tell your senior/RM right away; contact the client transparently, apologise, and explain the fix; then work the correction (reverse or redeem the excess) and document it. Reward honesty + urgency + escalation + client transparency. Any hint of concealing it or quietly hoping it slides is a serious red flag for this role.
